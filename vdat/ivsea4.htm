Interview with Sea4
by Cicatrix
[January 1998]

How did you start out in computers?

    Well, when I was about 5 or 6 we got a commodor 64. I still have it, and it works, even though its held together with electrical tape. I used play a few games on it, and even did a little BASIC. Of course I just typed in the examples from the manual, but it was fun. Then we got a NES, and I never thought about what I liked to consider real computers for a long time. And then Christmas '94 we got an AST advantage, I still use the monitor :) The only reason I ever even learned how to change Directories was to play DOOM. All of my early learning was based around DOOM, I would simply learn stuff to help me play doom and edit levels. Then one glorious day... I learned about EDIT.com, and haven't slowed down since.

How and when did you start out in the virus scene?

    Well, I want to be a programmer when I get out of college. So I thought I might as well start early and got a book on 'C', that was interesting but not much fun. So I borrowed a few books on ASM from a friend who got them in a 'Ten books for 10 cents deal'. It was really easy to understand and a whole lot of fun, so I thought I was getting somewhere. Then I saw a messageon one of the NGs for the Power Empire. Sounded interesting... originally I felt like doing Cracking, but after reading Dark Angels funky virus writing guide, I had found my calling :) This was only a few months ago, but I picked it up pretty fast.

Have you written viruses? If so which would you like to take credit for?

    Yes, I have. To name a few... Simple, Nautilus, Occido, And my latest Basho. But those are without the example virii for my tuts and i made a couple unreleased ones. Mostly involving encryption.

How do you name your viruses?

    Nautilus was from 20,000 Leagues Under the Sea. A few of the examples and Occido were words from Latin ( and they said it was useless :). Also, Basho wrote a Haiku that hangs on the wall in my school, its included in the virus.

Which programming languages do you know?

    Well, I know some BASIC. I am pretty good at C, but never really learned all the functions, I understand the syntax though. And ASM, which is by far more fun than anything :)

What programming language do you like using the most?

    As I mentioned above ASM is really great. It allows you to be the God of your CPU. And you control every single thing, really down and dirty. C, and BASIC are shitty in that respect.

Are you a member of a VX group?

    Yep, I am a proud member of the CodeBreakers.

Which AV software do you like/respect the most? Which the least?

    F-Prot and TBav are really nice. TBav gets pissed at every little thing I do :) I like a good AV product, it poses a challenge and they don't hurt me in any way. Of course, I hate the 30 day trial periods. McAffee sucks, I am pretty sure a chia pet has better heuristic capability.

What are your goals (VX wise)?

    Umm.. To know everything there is to know, and master it. Pretty humble eh? Well, I want to write some great encryption stuff, like tuts and examples. Encryption seems to be the most fascinating aspect of virii for me.

What is your view on the continuous 'war' between VX and AV.

    Haha, they make money off of our existence. And they act like they are mad at us. No way, I'll bet they have all written or at least thought about writing virii.

Where did you get you handle? What does it mean?

    Sea4... Well, when I got my AST we had AOL. After my brother was an idiot and got us kicked off, we signed up with an ISP. After spending a few hours thinking of a good name I thought of Sea4 ( or C4 ). I changed the 'C' to a Sea because its more creative and makes ya think. Plus I really love nature, and the Ocean is pretty much the biggest symbol of it around. Also, C4 was too short :) Then after we got seperate accounts with another ISP I kept the Sea4.

What is your view on Virus Creation software (eg. VCL, PS-MPC etc.)?

    Hmm, sound pretty weak to me. For lamers who just want to make a virus and claim that they can make virii. As far as making a virus in one of them to see how a particular thing like encryption or polymorphism is done, I suppose its a good learning tool.

What is your view on macro viruses vs. assembly or HLL viruses?

    Well, I am not really knowledgeable about Macro virii, but it seems like we aren't playing fair by making documents unsafe. Of course I still write in TXT so I have no worries. As for HLLs, its just plain silly. Its like trying to win the Indy 500 with a hearse.

Have you ever confirmed one of your viruses 'in-the-wild'?

    Nope, I don't release Compiled code. If anyone has actually compiled some of my source and infected people, I would be interested to know about it.

Which VX E-zine do you like to most? Which the least?

    Hehe, I am gonna have to say our magazine. It starts from the ground up. I read a few others and they don't seem to realize, some people never did any vx work before.

Which individual or what group do you like/respect in the VX world?

    Dark Angel, he has a great tutorial for beginners. Not perfect, but still it was how I learned and I recommend it to anyone.

In the AV world?

    TBav, and F-Prot creators. Very nice products you have there.

Which individual or what group do you like/respect outside the VX or the AV world?

    Anyone who desires to learn and know the truth.

What is your view on destructive payloads in viruses?

    I haven't made any. I don't reccommend others make any. Its wrong to destroy things that aren't yours. Its much more creative to confuse people. My first idea in virii was to create a virus that encrypted TXT files. I actually had a working copy, but it wasn't good enough for me, so I scratched it. Maybe I'll remake it later. The way it worked was reversible, I felt it would be a new thing for the AV software to decrypt encrypted texts.

Do you think there is such a thing as a 'good' virus?

    Wasn't there a virus that compressed files. I think it was called 'crunch'. Sounds pretty good to me. Yes, as long as it is benefical, and doesn't go off one day and do something destructive. If a virus does nothing destructive or constructive it isn't bad or good. Its indifferent. :)

What do you do in 'real' life?

    Real life?? I go to school and get bad grades because I am too lazy to do real work. I hang out with friends, contrary to common AV belief that all vx people are loners.

Do people outside the VX scene know what you do (parents, girlfriend etc.)?

    Yes, a few of em. At first they get all freaked because the media has them believe we are on the same level with serial killers, but when you explain the difference between malicious and the true learners, they calm down a bit. A couple of my peers, who saw 'Hackers', but not 'Wargames' think its cool to "hack the planet", even though I have no hacking experience.

Do you do other computer stuff outside VX (Hacking, phreaking, warez etc.)?

    I tried Cracking, no luck. I basically just fix peoples coms for free because no one will hire me and I think they are getting plastered by people with college degrees. The guy who fixes coms at school is not very favourable towards me anymore since I can fix things before he can even respond to the call "Help, How do I create an icon?".

Should viruses be illegal? Is there a difference between creation and spreading?

    No, viruses should not be illeagal. Spreading should be though. If a virus destroys data, legal action should be taken. If it is a harmless thing that nobody would ever notice. Let it be.

Describe the perfect virus.

    Hehe, it would contain the string 'Sea4, [CodeBreakers]'. Just kidding. Nope, it would have to be 100% undetectable, would infect computers for 10 years, announce itself on them all, and disinfect itself. Saying something like... "Hehe, I beat you all!"

What is your view on Windows (95)

    I much prefer DOS, in fact... I have my DOS prompt open 75% of the time. I do all my small file maintenance from there, program virii, write and read tuts, and other various tasks. The only good thing about W95 is that they released a new version of MS-DOS editor with it.

What is your advice for people just starting out?

    Read DA's funky virus writing tut.

Where can you be reached if at all?

    Sea4@ThePentagon.com, I am starting to hang out on IRC, Undernet, #virus. You can look for me there.

Any greets?

    All the CodeBreakers, Spo0ky, Arsonic, Opic, Duke, Aftershox, and Bach plus all the new ones by the time this is printed.

Any other comments?

    Through learning is gained respect for knowledge.

How did you get into Codebreakers?

    When Spo0ky decided to leave the power empire, I went with him. This was only a few days after I started.

How is Codebreakers organized?

    Spo0ky is the leader, Arsonic is the co-leader, Toad does a job of handling the mag, Bach does MAC stuff. I just answer questions about encryption, I am the 'Encryption Man' I think.

What is in your future and the future of Codebreakers?

    Well, I am writing a series on everything I know and can find out about encryption for the mag. I spend alot of time reading tuts on encryption and hypothisizing on different methods.

Will Codebreakers increase the number of members?

    Yes, if there are competent, willing people.

How's Codebreaker Zine #3 coming along?

    Great, I have the second part of my Encryption tut almost done. They are putting in Basho. I am writing a tut on DEBUG, and commenting a TSR virus. As for the other CBs, I am not sure. Toad is writing a TSR tut, and thats about all I can confirm.
