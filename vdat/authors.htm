After a dramatic rise in the number of virus authors during the late 1990's several highly publicized virus outbreaks and resulting law enforcement involvement have caused a large number of authors to either quit or "lay low". Some well known names from the early computer virus era have left the scene or have aimed their efforts on other computer related activities, often due to lack of interest. After the post Melissa scare and decline in activity amongst authors a definite increase in activity, the number of new virus projects and authors could be observed late 1999. There are signs that somewhere in the near future the number of actively virus producing individuals will reach an equilibrium, the number of people "dropping out" of the scene seems close to the number of new identities appearing on the scene. Apparently the media hypes and the resulting law enforcement involvement have a limiting effect on the number of people willing to put up with the perceived risks.

Earlier when a source code of a virus was "released" either intentionally or unintentionally, budding, not so capable, virus authors could not resist the temptation of slightly changing the code and inserting their own name/handle and thus creating an immense number of variants of a particular virus strain. Some people with no clue at all just inserted their name/handle without changing anything else, ran the source through a shareware compiler/linker and uploaded the resulting virus to a VX site or some popular newsgroup. At the same time an increasing number of virus creation tools became available to an increasing number of people which resulted in a floodwave of viruses all signed by their "creators".

Below you will find a list of "handles" that I found floating around in cyberspace until now. Many virus authors change their "handles" at least once in the course of their "career", some have a whole stack of them. Since it is almost impossible to keep track of this flow in names the list below contains "handles" that may belong to the same author. No effort was made to link these in any way.

As most of you may already know, there is no apparent lack of imagination coming up with these image making "handles". No special ASCII characters were used in the list below (eg. Talon, Kohntark etc.). Furthermore the intermittent and erratic use of caps was disregarded.

This list is by no means exhaustive / complete and does not even reflect all the handles mentioned elsewhere in VDAT, it is just meant to show the variety and multitude of names / handles that are used.
